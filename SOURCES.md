# Sources & References

## Books

### Foundational Texts

#### Parapsychological Research
- **Roll, W.G.** (1972). *The Poltergeist*. Scarecrow Press. ISBN: 978-0810804784
  - Foundational text on RSPK (Recurrent Spontaneous Psychokinesis) theory
  - Analysis of multiple cases from parapsychological perspective
  - Essential reading for understanding RSPK framework

- **Gauld, A. & Cornell, A.D.** (1979). *Poltergeists*. Routledge & Kegan Paul. ISBN: 978-0710000378
  - Comprehensive scholarly analysis of poltergeist phenomena
  - Historical survey of cases
  - Classification systems for phenomena types
  - Critical academic approach

- **Houran, J. & Lange, R.** (Eds.) (2001). *Hauntings and Poltergeists: Multidisciplinary Perspectives*. McFarland. ISBN: 978-0786409358
  - Academic collection of perspectives from multiple disciplines
  - Psychological, parapsychological, and skeptical viewpoints
  - Current research methodologies

### Case Studies

#### Enfield Poltergeist
- **Playfair, G.L.** (1980). *This House Is Haunted: The True Story of the Enfield Poltergeist*. Souvenir Press. ISBN: 978-0285626751
  - Primary account by SPR investigator Guy Lyon Playfair
  - Detailed documentation of 18-month investigation
  - Most famous modern poltergeist case
  - Essential primary source

#### Other Notable Cases
- **Wilson, C.** (1982). *Poltergeist! A Study in Destructive Haunting*. New English Library. ISBN: 978-0450042119
  - Popular overview of famous poltergeist cases
  - Accessible introduction to phenomenon
  - Historical and modern cases

- **Fodor, N.** (1958). *On the Trail of the Poltergeist*. Citadel Press.
  - Accounts of Fodor's investigations
  - Psychoanalytic approach to poltergeist phenomena
  - Historical perspective

- **Owen, A.R.G.** (1964). *Can We Explain the Poltergeist?* Garrett Publications.
  - Early attempt at comprehensive explanation
  - Multiple theoretical frameworks examined

### Historical Accounts

- **Glanvill, J.** (1681). *Saducismus Triumphatus: Or, Full and Plain Evidence Concerning Witches and Apparitions*.
  - Contains account of Tedworth Drummer case (1661-1663)
  - Historical documentation
  - Early investigation attempt

- **Ingram, M.V.** (1894). *An Authenticated History of the Bell Witch*. 
  - Primary historical source for Bell Witch case
  - Written decades after events
  - Folkloric and historical elements

- **Bell, C.** (1985). *The Bell Witch: A Mysterious Spirit*. 
  - Later analysis of Bell Witch case
  - Additional documentation and research

### Skeptical Analysis

- **Nickell, J.** (2012). *The Science of Ghosts: Searching for Spirits of the Dead*. Prometheus Books. ISBN: 978-1616145972
  - Skeptical scientific analysis
  - Debunking of famous cases
  - Investigation methodologies
  - Critical thinking approaches

- **Kaplan, S.** (1995). *The Amityville Horror Conspiracy*. Belfry Books. ISBN: 978-0964230903
  - Exposé of Amityville hoax
  - Investigation of fraud
  - Cautionary tale

- **Dingwall, E.J., Goldney, K.M., & Hall, T.H.** (1956). *The Haunting of Borley Rectory: A Critical Survey of the Evidence*. Society for Psychical Research.
  - SPR investigation contradicting Harry Price's claims
  - Critical analysis of investigator fraud
  - Methodological importance

### General Paranormal Context

- **Rogo, D.S.** (1979). *The Poltergeist Experience*. Penguin.
  - Overview of poltergeist phenomena
  - Theoretical frameworks
  - Case collections

- **Randles, J. & Hough, P.** (1993). *The Afterlife: An Investigation into the Mysteries of Life After Death*. Berkley.
  - Broader context of survival research
  - Poltergeists within afterlife hypothesis

## Academic Papers & Research

### Parapsychological Research

- **Roll, W.G.** (1977). "Poltergeists." In B. Wolman (Ed.), *Handbook of Parapsychology* (pp. 382-413). Van Nostrand Reinhold.
  - Comprehensive academic overview of RSPK research
  - Theoretical framework
  - Research methodologies

- **Bender, H.** (1969). "Modern Poltergeist Research: A Plea for an Unprejudiced Approach." In *Proceedings of the Parapsychological Association* (Vol. 5, pp. 33-34).
  - Scientific investigation of Rosenheim Poltergeist
  - Physics-based approach
  - Collaboration with physicists

- **Roll, W.G.** (1972). "The Poltergeist and PK." In *Proceedings of the Parapsychological Association* (Vol. 9, pp. 107-108).
  - Psychokinesis and poltergeist connection
  - RSPK theory development

- **Gauld, A.** (1970). "A Series of 'Drop-in' Communicators." *Proceedings of the Society for Psychical Research* (Vol. 55, pp. 273-340).
  - Related research on spirit communication
  - Comparative analysis

- **Owen, I.M. & Sparrow, M.** (1976). *Conjuring Up Philip: An Adventure in Psychokinesis*. Harper & Row.
  - Experimental creation of "artificial" poltergeist
  - Group psychokinesis research
  - Philip Experiment

### Neuropsychological Perspectives

- **Persinger, M.A.** (1988). "Increased Geomagnetic Activity and the Occurrence of Bereavement Hallucinations: Evidence for Melatonin-Mediated Microseizuring in the Temporal Lobe?" *Neuroscience Letters* (Vol. 88, pp. 271-274).
  - Temporal lobe and paranormal experiences
  - Geomagnetic correlations

- **Persinger, M.A. & Koren, S.A.** (2001). "Predicting the Characteristics of Haunt Phenomena from Geomagnetic Factors and Brain Sensitivity." In J. Houran & R. Lange (Eds.), *Hauntings and Poltergeists: Multidisciplinary Perspectives* (pp. 179-194). McFarland.
  - Environmental factors in poltergeist cases
  - Brain sensitivity and electromagnetic fields

### Psychological Research

- **Huesmann, L.R. & Podolski, C.P.** (2003). "Punishment: A Psychological Perspective." In S. McConville (Ed.), *The Use of Punishment* (pp. 55-88). Willan Publishing.
  - Relevant to stress and adolescent behavior
  - Psychological context for RSPK agents

- **Radin, D.I.** (1997). *The Conscious Universe: The Scientific Truth of Psychic Phenomena*. HarperOne.
  - Meta-analysis of psychokinesis research
  - Statistical approaches
  - Relevance to RSPK theory

### Skeptical Research

- **Wiseman, R., Watt, C., Stevens, P., Greening, E., & O'Keeffe, C.** (2003). "An Investigation into Alleged 'Hauntings'." *British Journal of Psychology* (Vol. 94, pp. 195-211).
  - Psychological factors in haunting experiences
  - Environmental influences
  - Suggestion and expectation

- **French, C.C., Haque, U., Bunton-Stasyshyn, R., & Davis, R.** (2009). "The 'Haunt' Project: An Attempt to Build a 'Haunted' Room by Manipulating Complex Electromagnetic Fields and Infrasound." *Cortex* (Vol. 45, pp. 619-629).
  - Experimental creation of haunting experiences
  - Environmental manipulation
